The Formula Explained

The calculator above uses this formula, which reflects the real cost stack for a dropshipping sale:

Net Profit = Sale Price − Product Cost − Shipping − Platform Fee − Ad Spend

Net Margin % = (Net Profit ÷ Sale Price) × 100

Break-even ROAS = Sale Price ÷ Ad Spend

Let's walk through each component:

Product Cost (COGS)

This is the price you pay your supplier per unit. On AliExpress or CJ Dropshipping, this is the listed price per item. Don't confuse it with your landed cost — shipping is a separate line item because it varies by fulfilment method and changes as you scale.

Shipping Cost

For CN-to-AU dropshipping, common rates are: AliExpress ePacket $4–$7, CJ Packet $6–$10, DHL Express $18–$28. If you're using a local AU fulfilment centre (3PL), your shipping per order might be $8–$12 domestically but your landed inventory cost increases. Always model the actual shipping method you plan to use — not the cheapest option that may result in 25-day delivery times.

Platform Fee

Every platform charges a percentage of sale price plus a fixed amount per transaction. Common rates:

  • Shopify + Shopify Payments AU: 2.9% + $0.30 (basic plan)
  • Shopify + third-party gateway: 2.9% + 0.5–2% additional
  • eBay AU: 13.5% (no fixed fee, but high percentage)
  • Amazon AU: 8–15% depending on category
  • WooCommerce + Stripe AU: 1.7% + $0.30 (lower, but you pay hosting)

Ad Spend as a % of Revenue

This is where most sellers underestimate costs. At scale, paid traffic (Meta, TikTok Ads, Google Shopping) typically costs 20–40% of revenue. A new store testing a product will likely spend 35–50% of revenue on ads before finding the right creative. Set your ad spend percentage to what you expect at steady state, not your worst-case testing phase — but be honest about what "steady state" means for your store.

What a Healthy Margin Looks Like

There are no universal rules, but here is how most experienced dropshippers interpret their margins:

  • Net margin above 25%: Strong. This product can sustain scaling and absorb ad cost variance. You have room to test creative, absorb a bad week, and still profit.
  • Net margin 15–25%: Viable. Profitable, but thin. A 10% rise in CPM or a bad product batch will compress this quickly. Only scale products in this range once you have proven creative.
  • Net margin below 15%: Danger zone. One spike in ad costs or a 5% return rate pushes this negative. Raise your price or find a cheaper supplier before scaling.
  • Break-even ROAS above 3×: High risk. Most ad accounts sustain 2–2.5× ROAS at scale. If your break-even ROAS is 3× or higher, your margin relies on exceptional ad performance that is difficult to sustain.

GST and Its Effect on Your Margin

If your store exceeds $75,000 AUD in annual turnover, you must register for GST in Australia. Once registered, 1/11th of each sale is GST that you collect and remit to the ATO — it is not your revenue. A product selling for $55 AUD includes $5 of GST (if GST-registered). Your effective revenue per sale is $50, not $55.

The calculator above uses the sale price as-entered. If you are GST-registered, enter your ex-GST sale price (sale price ÷ 1.1) in the Sale Price field to get accurate net profit. Read our guide on best dropshipping products for Australia for more on AU-specific tax considerations.

The 3× Rule: Quick Sanity Check

Before running the full calculator, use this quick heuristic: your sale price should be at least 3× your landed cost (product + shipping). Below that multiple, it is very difficult to generate positive net margin at typical AU ad spend rates. This is why product selection matters — if a product's market price is only 2× your landed cost, move on.
